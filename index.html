Mehdi Zahmatkesh

Independent Neuroscience Researcher

From an early age, I have been deeply curious about the human mind and the mechanisms behind behavior and emotion. This curiosity gradually evolved into an independent path of research exploring psychology, neuroscience, and the transformative role of narrative and identity in human experience.

My personal encounters with anxiety and panic attacks also became an important turning point in my intellectual journey. They motivated me to study the psychological and neurocognitive mechanisms behind panic, emotional regulation, and behavioral change.

Over the years, I have explored topics such as self-hypnosis, behavioral change, emotional regulation, addiction recovery, and the relationship between narrative identity and neural processes.

Research Philosophy

My research explores how narrative identity, cognitive processes, and neural mechanisms interact to shape human behavior. I am particularly interested in how internal narratives influence emotional regulation, behavioral change, and the reconstruction of identity-related neural pathways.
